SWEET POTATO & BLACK BEAN STEW

with Monterey Jack & Jalapeño


READY IN: 40 MIN

PREP IN: 10 MIN


INGREDIENTS

Black Beans

(1/2)

Southwest Spice

(2/4)

Shredded Monterey Jack

(1/2)

Sour Cream

(1/2)

Jalapeño

(1/2)

Tomato Paste

(1/2)


Veggie Stock Concentrate

(1/2)

Yellow Onion

(1/2)

Sweet Potato

(1/2)

Tex-Mex Paste

(1/2 tsp)

Garlic

(2/4 Cloves)

Cooking Oil

(2/4 tsp)


CHEF's TIP

Want to take this stew to the next level? Just add CRUNCH! If you have some tortilla chips hanging out in your pantry, crush 'em up and sprinkle over the top as you eat. (You can thank us later.)


INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Wash and dry all produce. Halve, peel, and dice onion. Thinly slice half the jalapeño into rounds; finely chop remaining jalapeño. (Remove ribs and seeds first for less heat.) Peel and finely chop garlic. Dice sweet potato 5-7 minutes. into ½-inch pieces (peel first if desired).

  2. Heat a large drizzle of oil in a medium pot over medium-high heat. Add onion and chopped jalapeño to taste; season with salt and pepper. Cook, stirring, until softened,

  3. Add sweet potato to pot and season generously with salt and pepper. Cook, stirring, until lightly browned, 4-6 minutes. Add garlic and Southwest Spice; cook, stirring, until fragrant, 30-60 seconds.

  4. Stir in tomato paste and Tex-Mex paste. Cook until slightly darkened and combined, 1-2 minutes. Stir in beans and their liquid, stock concentrate, 1 cup water (2 cups for 4 servings), salt, and pepper. Bring to a simmer and cook until thickened, 12-15 minutes. Turn off heat. Taste and season with salt and pepper.

  5. Divide stew between bowls. Top with cheese, sour cream, and as much sliced jalapeño as you'd like.
